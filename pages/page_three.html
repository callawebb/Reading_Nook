Favorite Bookstores

A Good Bookstore A good bookstore is defined by how people appreciate it and find what they are loking for within it's walls. Not just books, but comfort, amusement, entertainment, and community.

My Favorites

My favorite bookstores spread far and wide. When I travel I love to sort through a bookstore to get a sense of their culture and community. Picking a bookstore aligned with your interests is important because each bookstroe markets themselves differently and carries a different voice.

My List: Magers and Quin- A Minneapolis staple I have been frequenting for years. Birchbark Books- Owned by National Book Award Winner Louise Erdrich. Carries a strong voices for women, nature, and the marginalized. Barnes and Noble- Sometimes you just want to know what everyone is reading. Black Garnet Books- The only black owned bookstore in the Twin Cities provides a platform for many voices. Wild Rumpus- Nestled into Linden Hills, this bookstore brings the charm and a plethora of nearby reading nooks Thriftbooks.com-you don't have to spend an arm and a leg to induldge in knowledge. They are always reliable and have a great selection. Libraries- A gift that keeps on giving. I'm always thankfully surprised with their contemporary selections, as well.

A thought.....

Finding your favorite bookstore is personal and is a journey. I hope you find the one that suits your reading desires and helps you find community.
